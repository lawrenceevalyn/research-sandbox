• Abstract
    This paper examines more than five hundred pre-Victorian Gothic works to ask whether female writers created categorically different works than their male peers. Two qualitative bibliographies of the Gothic provide “motifs” and “Gothic types” for computational modelling. In these models, many of the literary features which have been attributed to the ‘Female Gothic’ or to the ‘school of Radcliffe’ are, in fact, particular to Ann Radcliffe herself.
